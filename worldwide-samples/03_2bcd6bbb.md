# ECB names Joe Root interim captain as Stokes ruled out of New Zealand Test

*The England & Wales Cricket Board confirmed Ben Stokes and Gus Atkinson are unavailable for the second Test, appointing Joe Root as interim captain.*

`Hybrid A (try 1) · 297 words · verify=pass`

---

The England & Wales Cricket Board (ECB) issued a statement confirming that, because of an ongoing investigation, all‑rounder Ben Stokes and fast bowler Gus Atkinson will not be selected for the Rothesay second Test against New Zealand. The match is scheduled to begin at the Kia Oval on Wednesday 17 June.  

In the same announcement, the ECB named Yorkshire batter Joe Root as interim captain for the fixture. “Yorkshire batter Joe Root will lead the team as Interim Captain,” the board said. The decision follows the board’s assessment that the two players cannot be made available while the investigation proceeds.  

Stokes, who has been a mainstay of England’s Test side, responded to the development in a separate comment. He reflected on the demands of his role, saying, “Over a long period of time, you bowl a lot of overs, you’re standing in the field, you’re thinking about captaining and batting is mentally tiring as well. You then come off the field, and you’re getting that off and then getting this on. Over time, it can get a bit tiring.”  

The ECB’s statement was reiterated in two further releases, each confirming the same unavailability of Stokes and Atkinson and the appointment of Root. No further details of the investigation were disclosed.  

Root, who previously captained England in limited‑overs formats, will assume the leadership duties for the upcoming Test, a role the board described as interim. The decision comes as England prepare to face a New Zealand side that secured the Women’s T20 World Cup title in 2024, though the two teams compete in separate formats.  

The board’s clarification leaves the England squad for the second Test without two of its senior players, prompting adjustments to the team composition and strategy ahead of the match at the Kia Oval.
