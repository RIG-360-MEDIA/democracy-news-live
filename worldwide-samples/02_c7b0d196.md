# Ebola outbreak in DRC’s Ituri province intensifies as cases surge

*WHO and Africa CDC report over 500 confirmed infections and rising deaths, prompting cross‑border health measures and international aid.*

`Hybrid A (try 1) · 526 words · verify=pass`

---

The Ebola outbreak declared in the Democratic Republic of Congo on 15 May has accelerated, with health authorities confirming a sharp increase in cases across Ituri province. Africa’s top public‑health agency, the Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention (Africa CDC), reported a total of **between 282 and 544 confirmed cases** in the country, while the World Health Organization (WHO) listed **906 suspected cases** and **223 suspected deaths** linked to the Bundibugyo strain. Confirmed fatalities from the strain number **61**, bringing the overall death toll to **between 61 and 223** depending on classification.  

The surge has been concentrated in Ituri, where **more than 90 percent of reported cases** are located, according to Congolese authorities. The province’s proximity to Uganda has amplified cross‑border concerns. Uganda has recorded **19 confirmed Ebola cases**, most of them linked to travel from the DRC, and has already established two border laboratories in Mpondwe and Arua to support testing. “The number of people [health workers] who will be needed is a moving target but we know that we shall be sending batches of 40 teams per time … we think Uganda will be setting up about four treatment units,” said Daniel Kyabayinze, a senior official involved in the response.  

In response to the expanding threat, Africa CDC has called for stronger cross‑border preparedness. “Experts advising the Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention have called for stronger cross‑border preparedness in Africa to reduce the risk of imported cases from the Bundibugyo Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and Uganda,” the agency stated.  

International partners have mobilised additional resources. India dispatched a second tranche of **43 tonnes** of medical assistance to the Africa CDC, including protective gear, diagnostic equipment and medicines, to bolster Ebola response capacities across the continent. “Confident that this 43‑tonne consignment will further strengthen public health preparedness and bolster Ebola response capacities across Africa,” said Indian External Affairs Minister S Jaishankar.  

Funding for vaccine development has also been secured. The Coalition for Epidemic Preparedness Innovations (CEPI) pledged up to **$60 million** to Moderna and two other groups to accelerate the development of shots against the Bundibugyo strain, complementing an earlier commitment of **$50 million** for pre‑clinical and early‑clinical work on Moderna’s investigational BDBV vaccine candidate.  

Despite these measures, the outbreak remains “far from under control and continues to spread,” warned Africa CDC on 12 June. The agency estimates that **more than 20 000 contacts** may need monitoring, yet only **4 955 have been identified** so far.  

The escalating situation has prompted travel and entry restrictions. The United States announced that members of the Congolese football squad slated for a World Cup match must undergo a **21‑day isolation period** before entry, and Canada introduced temporary border measures to reduce the risk of the virus entering the country.  

Health officials stress that containment will depend on sustained surveillance, rapid case identification and coordinated cross‑border action. As the virus continues to move through densely populated communities in Ituri and across national frontiers, the combined efforts of national governments, regional bodies and international donors will be critical to curbing what remains one of the most severe Ebola outbreaks in recent years.
