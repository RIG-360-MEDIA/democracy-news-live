SpaceX Raises $75 Billion in Record U.S. IPO

The rocket company priced shares at $135, achieving a valuation of roughly $1.75 trillion despite posting a net loss last year.

By Rig Wire · 2 min read · 16 Jun 2026

SpaceX raised $75 billion in its initial public offering on Thursday, setting a final price of $135 per share. The offering values the Texas-based company at roughly $1.75 trillion, making it the largest IPO in history. The company is offering about 555.6 million shares, with 30% of the shares set aside for retail buyers.

The valuation reflects investor enthusiasm for Elon Musk’s ambitions in space, communications, and artificial intelligence, rather than the company's recent financial performance. SpaceX reported revenue of $18.7 billion last year but incurred a net loss of nearly $5 billion. Between the start of 2025 and March 31, 2026, the company lost $8.7 billion.

"I gave SpaceX less than a 10% chance of succeeding at all, to be clear," Musk said. "It is certainly hard to believe that a little company that started in a warehouse in El Segundo is now going public with the largest IPO ever."

SpaceX stated that its mission includes addressing the digital divide by aiming to connect over three billion unconnected people to the internet. The company also noted it has been responsible for more than 80% of the mass launched into orbit globally over the past three years.

The IPO price is significantly higher than some analyst estimates. Morningstar calculated that SpaceX is worth only $63 a share. "At a $1.75 trillion valuation, investors are betting more on future execution than current fundamentals," said Ishan Tanna. Michael Field added: "We believe the business has real strengths, particularly in Starlink, but with so many unknown and untested technologies underpinning much of the valuation price, particularly within the AI business, we think the valuation is extremely speculative."

Investor demand exceeded $250 billion. "The level of interest around SpaceX feels less like a normal IPO inquiry and more like investors trying to secure a seat before the rocket leaves the launch pad," said Hebe Chen.

Musk holds a stake valued at $866 billion and will control 82.4% of the voting power after the IPO. His net worth has surpassed $1.1 trillion, making him the first person in history to achieve a net worth exceeding $1 trillion.

SpaceX is set to begin trading on Nasdaq on Friday, June 12. Nasdaq Inc. recently changed its rules to allow SpaceX to join the Nasdaq 100 Index in just 15 trading days.
